Clearance

DAFFARS Subpart 5301.90 establishes the clearance definitions, objectives, approval thresholds, and procedures.

Objectives

Per DAFFARS 5301.9001(a) the objectives of the business and contract clearance process are to ensure that:

1

Acquisition Strategies

Negotiations and contract actions effectively implement approved acquisition strategies;

2

Business Arrangements

Negotiations and contract actions result in fair and reasonable business arrangements;

3

Consistent

Negotiations and contract actions are consistent with laws, regulations, and policies; and

4

Independent Review

An independent review and assessment by the clearance authority for the proposed contract action is accomplished.

Definitions

Business and contract clearances are defined in detail in DAFFARS 5301.9000. Generally, clearance can be defined as follows:

Competition Type Business Clearance Contract Clearance
Competitive Approval to issue the solicitation. Without discussions: Approval by CAA for the SSA to make the decision to award
With discussions: (1) Approval by the CAA for the SSA to request final proposal revisions in accordance with FAR 15.307; and
(2) Approval by the CAA for the SSA to make a source selection decision.
Non-competitive Approval to begin negotiations. Approval by the CAA to award the contract action.
DoD Peer Reviews [DFARS PGI 201.170]
DoD Peer Reviews are required for actions ≥1B at each of the business and contract clearance phases.

“Clearance Review” means the independent review performed by the designated clearance review office—or as otherwise selected by the CAA—before the clearance by the CAA. When ≥$10M and <$1B, see the Buyer Resource Tool (BRT)—tab 59—for local policies (i.e. committee review). When ≥$1B, see DAFFARS MP5301.90 for SAF/AQC coordination.

Clearance Approval Authority (CAA)

Approval Threshold Clearance Approval Authority (CAA)
≥$1B HCA—SAF/AQC
>$500M to <$1B SCO
>$100M to ≤$500M One Level Below the SCO—COCO
≥$10M to ≤$100M Two Levels Below the SCO—Branch Chief

“Clearance Approval Authority (CAA)” must be distinct from the contracting officer responsible for the contract action. When a contracting official is the Source Selection Authority (SSA), the CAA must be a level above the contracting official. If the SCO or their deputy is the SSA, the CAA must be the cognizant HCA.
