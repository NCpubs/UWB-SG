Fiscal Law

Three Tenants of Fiscal Law

Purpose

Misappropriation Acti

Appropriated funds may be used only for the objects (e.g., purposes and programs) for which appropriations were made
(e.g., "Colors of Money").

Time

Bona Fide Need Ruleii

Appropriated funds may be used only for goods and services for which a need arises during the period of that appropriation’s availability for obligation.

Amount

Anti Deficiency Actiii

Executive agencies cannot:

  1. Obligate more funds than available in an appropriation or in a formal subdivision of funds.
  2. Make obligations that exceed the amount permitted by agency actions/regulations.
  3. Obligate funds in advance of receiving an appropriation or allotment.

There are several exceptions to the Bona Fide Need Ruleii:

  • Supply items:
    • Lead time: Agencies are permitted to consider normal lead time in determining bona fide need.
    • Stock level: Agencies may use current year funds to replace stock consumed.
  • Service contracts:
    • Non-severable: Agencies may fund the entire non-severable effort with budget available for obligation at the time the contract is awarded.
    • Severable: Agencies may obligate funds available at contract award to finance a severable service contract with a period of performance not to exceed 12 months.
  • Project Orders:
    • The DoD Financial Management Regulation (FMR) provides for a year-end exception for project orders (e.g., maintenance and repair). A project will be considered to have met the bona fide need of the prior fiscal year if work is started by the end of the calendar year.

Appropriation Lifecycle

Each appropriation category has three distinct periods during its lifecycle:iv

Current

Funds are available for new obligations, obligation adjustments, expenditures, and outlays.

The current period varies by appropriation category:

Category Current Period
O&M (3400) 1 year
RDT&E (3600) 2 years
Procurement
(3010, 3020, 3080, etc.)		 3 years
MILCON (3300) 5 years

Expired

Funds are available for obligation adjustments, expenditures, and outlays (but no new obligations).

The expired period duration

— 5 years —

is the same for all appropriations.

Cancelled

Regardless of appropriation category, funds are unavailable for obligations, adjustments, expenditures, and outlays.

Since Program Offices cannot use appropriated funds in the cancelled period to pay legitimate invoices, Programs Offices can only consider use of current period funds.

Appropriation Categories Summary

Expenses are the costs incurred to operate and maintain the organization. Investments are the costs that result in the acquisition of, or an addition to, end items that benefit future periods and generally are of a long-term character.

  • Operations and Maintenance (O&M)
    • Expenses including:
    • O&M appropriations traditionally do not finance investments, but rather those things whose benefits are derived for a limited period of time (i.e., expenses).
    • Examples of costs financed by O&M funds are headquarters operations, travel, fuel, minor construction projects less than $4M, expenses of operational military forces, training and education, recruiting, depot maintenance, purchases from Defense Working Capital Funds (e.g., spare parts), base operations support, and assets with a system unit cost less than the current expense/investment threshold ($350K).
  • Research, Development, Test and Evaluaton (RDT&E)
    • Investments and expenses, including:
    • Research, development, test and evaluation efforts.
    • End items, weapons, equipment, components, and materials as well as performance of services necessary to develop and test the system.
    • The operating costs of dedicated activities engaged in the conduct of R&D programs.
  • Procurement
    • Investments including:
    • Items whose system unit cost exceeds the current expense/investment threshold ($350K).
    • All centrally managed end items not purchased from Defense Working Capital Funds, regardless of unit cost (e.g., handguns); purchases from the Defense Working Capital Fund furnished as part of a system acquisition, system modification, major service life extension program and initial spares.
    • With certain limited exceptions, the cost of fabricating and installing additions or modifications to existing end items.
  • Military Construction (MILCON)
    • Investments including:
    • Major construction projects costing greater than $4M.
